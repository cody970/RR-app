## How MLC Mechanical Royalties Work

### The Royalty Flow

```
Spotify/Apple/Amazon/etc. (DSPs)
        │
        ├── Recording Royalties ──→ Distributor ──→ Artist (Sada Baby)
        │                                          ✅ COLLECTED via SoundExchange
        │
        ├── Performance Royalties ──→ PRO (BMI) ──→ Songwriter (Sada Baby)
        │                                          ✅ COLLECTED via BMI
        │
        └── Mechanical Royalties ──→ MLC ──→ Publisher ──→ Songwriter
                                              ⚠️ GAPS IDENTIFIED
```

### Key Facts
- Mechanical royalties are approximately **half** of all songwriter royalties from streaming
- The MLC collects from ALL US DSPs (Spotify, Apple Music, Amazon, Pandora, YouTube, SoundCloud, Tidal, etc.)
- The MLC takes **0% commission** — passes through 100% to members
- **Free to join** — membership costs nothing
- If a work is not registered with the MLC, the mechanical royalties become **"unmatched"** and sit in a pool
- After 3 years, unmatched royalties are distributed to existing members by **market share** (i.e., major publishers get it)

### What This Means for Sada Baby

If Sada Baby is only registered with BMI (performance royalties), he is potentially missing **half of his songwriter royalties** from streaming. The MLC handles the other half — mechanical royalties. Without proper MLC registration (either directly or through a publisher), those mechanicals are sitting in the MLC's unmatched pool.
