## The 732 vs. 99 Problem

Genius catalogs **732 songs** where Sada Baby is credited as a primary artist, featured artist, or contributor. ASCAP shows only **99 registered works**. Even accounting for the fact that not every Genius entry represents a unique composition where Sada Baby has a songwriting credit, the gap is staggering.

### Why the Gap Exists

1. **Prolific Output, Minimal Administration** — Sada Baby is described by Pitchfork as one of the "most prolific rappers of his generation." He releases music at an extraordinary rate, but publishing registration has not kept pace.

2. **Three Label Transitions** — Moving from Grizzley Gang (2017) to Asylum Records (2018) to Independent/Big Squad LLC (2021) and back to self-releasing creates administrative chaos. Each transition means different distributors, different publishing administrators, and different registration responsibilities.

3. **DatPiff/SoundCloud Era** — Projects like Whoop Tape (2019) and Brolik (2020) were released as DatPiff exclusives or SoundCloud drops. These platforms have zero publishing registration infrastructure.

4. **Feature-Heavy Catalog** — With 66+ documented features on other artists' tracks, the responsibility for registering Sada Baby's songwriting contribution often falls on the lead artist's team — and frequently doesn't happen.

5. **Self-Written Material** — As a rapper who writes his own lyrics, Sada Baby is both the performer AND songwriter. Many rappers in this position miss the publishing registration step entirely, collecting only the artist/master side of royalties.

## Conclusion

Sada Baby's catalog represents a textbook case of the "prolific artist, minimal administration" problem that plagues independent and transitioning artists in hip-hop. With **732 songs on Genius but only 99 ASCAP registrations**, the gap is one of the largest we have documented.

The financial impact is substantial: conservatively **$240K–$1.15M+** in recoverable royalties across ASCAP, The MLC, and SoundExchange. The majority of this recovery can be achieved through systematic registration of existing works — no new music needs to be created, just proper administration of what already exists.
